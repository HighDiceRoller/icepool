Notes

  • This does not take heroic successes into account.
  • If more dice are specified to be dropped and/or kept than there are in the pool, the excess kept dice do not contribute to the total.
  • "Drop" is used for e.g. the Burst SFX and removes the highest-rolling dice before keeping among the rest. In case of a tie, the smaller die is removed.
  • Remember that the second roller loses ties, dice that roll a 1 (hitch) can't be used for the total or effect, and there is always the option to have a "free" d4 effect.
  • On the "exactly" plot, the effect die curves show the chance of rolling exactly a total while keeping an effect die of at least that size.
  • On the "at least" plot, the vertical position of each effect die curve at a particular total is the chance of achieving that total while keeping an effect die of at least that size.
  • On the "at least" plot, the vertical extent of each effect die area at a particular total is the chance of achieving that total while keeping an effect die of exactly that size.
  • On the "at least" plot, the area above the d4 curve represents failure.

How does it work?

I built this using Pyodide, Chart.js, and of course, my own Icepool Python library.
