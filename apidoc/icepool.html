  17class Die():
  18    """An immutable mapping of outcomes to nonnegative `int` weights.
  19
  20    Dice are immutable. Methods do not modify the die in-place;
  21    rather they return a die representing the result.
  22
  23    It *is* (mostly) well-defined to have a die with zero-weight outcomes,
  24    even though this is not a proper probability distribution.
  25    These can be useful in a few cases, such as:
  26
  27    * `DicePool` and `EvalPool` will iterate through zero-weight outcomes
  28        with 0 `count`, rather than `None` or skipping that outcome.
  29    * `icepool.align()` and the like are convenient for making dice share the
  30        same set of outcomes.
  31
  32    However, zero-weight outcomes have a computational cost like any other
  33    outcome. Unless you have a specific use case in mind, it's best to leave
  34    them out.
  35
  36    Most operators and methods will not introduce zero-weight outcomes if their
  37    arguments do not have any.
  38
  39    It's also possible to have "empty" dice with no outcomes at all,
  40    though these have little use other than being sentinel values.
  41    """
  42
  43    def __init__(self,
  44                 *args,
  45                 weights=None,
  46                 min_outcome=None,
  47                 denominator_method='lcm'):
  48        """Constructor for a die..
  49
  50        Don't confuse this with `icepool.d()`:
  51
  52        * `icepool.Die(6)`: A die that always rolls the `int` 6.
  53        * `icepool.d(6)`: A d6.
  54
  55        Here are some different ways of constructing a d6:
  56
  57        * Just import it: `from icepool import d6`
  58        * Use the `d()` function: `icepool.d(6)`
  59        * Use a d6 that you already have: `Die(d6)`
  60        * Mix a d3 and a d3+3: `Die(d3, d3+3)`
  61        * Use a dict: `Die({1:1, 2:1, 3:1, 4:1, 5:1, 6:1})`
  62        * Give the faces as args: `Die(1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)`
  63
  64        All weights must be non-negative, though they can be zero.
  65
  66        Args:
  67            *args: Each of these arguments can be one of the following:
  68                * A die. The outcomes of the die will be "flattened" into the
  69                    result; a die object will never contain a die as an outcome.
  70                * A dict-like that maps outcomes to weights.
  71                    The outcomes of the dict-like will be "flattened" into the
  72                    result. This option will be taken in preference to treating
  73                    the dict-like  itself as an outcome even if the dict-like
  74                    itself is hashable and comparable.
  75                * A tuple of outcomes, which produces a joint distribution.
  76                    Any arguments that are dice or dicts will expand the tuple
  77                    according to their independent joint distribution.
  78                    For example, (d6, d6) will expand to 36 ordered tuples with
  79                    weight 1 each. Use this sparingly since it may create a
  80                    large number of outcomes.
  81                * `icepool.Reroll`, which will drop itself
  82                    and the corresponding element of `weights` from consideration.
  83                * Anything else will be treated as a single outcome.
  84                    These must be hashable and mutually comparable with all other
  85                    outcomes (after expansion). The same outcome can appear
  86                    multiple times, in which case it will be weighted
  87                    proportionally higher.
  88            weights: Controls the relative weight of the arguments.
  89                If an argument expands into multiple outcomes, the weight is
  90                shared among those outcomes. If not provided, each argument will
  91                end up  with the same total weight. For example, `Die(d6, 7)` is
  92                the same as `Die(1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 7, 7, 7, 7, 7)`.
  93            min_outcome: If used, there must be zero `*args`, and `weights` must
  94                be provided. The outcomes of the result will be integers
  95                starting at `min_outcome`,one per weight in `weights` with that
  96                weight.
  97            denominator_method: How to determine the denominator of the result
  98                if the arguments themselves contain weights. This is also used
  99                for dict-like arguments. From greatest to least:
 100                * 'prod': Product of the individual argument denominators, times
 101                    the total of `weights`. This is like rolling all of the
 102                    possible dice, and then selecting a result.
 103                * 'lcm' (default): LCM of the individual argument denominators,
 104                    times the total of `weights`. This is like rolling `weights`
 105                    first, then selecting an argument to roll.
 106                * 'lcm_weighted': LCM of the individual (argument denominators
 107                    times corresponding element of `weights`). This is like
 108                     rolling the above, but the specific weight rolled is used
 109                    to help determine the result of the selected argument.
 110                * 'reduce': `reduce()` is called at the end.
 111        Raises:
 112            `ValueError`: `None` is not a valid outcome for a die.
 113        """

 115                                     weights=weights,
 116                                     min_outcome=min_outcome,
 117                                     denominator_method=denominator_method)

 124    def unary_op(self, op, *args, **kwargs):
 125        """Performs the operation on the outcomes.
 126
 127        The result is a die.
 128
 129        This is used for the operators `-, +, abs, ~, round, trunc, floor, ceil, []`.
 130        """
 131        data = defaultdict(int)
 132        for outcome, weight in self.items():
 133            data[op(outcome, *args, **kwargs)] += weight
 134        return icepool.Die(data)
 135
 136    def binary_op(self, other, op, *args, **kwargs):
 137        """Performs the operation on pairs of outcomes.
 138
 139        The result is a die.
 140
 141        The other operand is cast to a die before performing the operation.
 142
 143        This is used for the operators
 144        `+, -, *, /, //, %, **, <<, >>, &, |, ^, <, <=, >=, >, ==, !=`.
 145        Note that `*` multiplies outcomes directly;
 146        it is not the same as `@` or `d()`.
 147
 148        Special operators:
 149            * The `@` operator rolls the left die, then rolls the right die that
 150                many times and sums the outcomes. Only the left side will be
 151                cast to a die; the right side must already be a die.
 152            * `==` and `!=` are applied across outcomes like the other
 153                operators. They also set the truth value of the die according to
 154                whether the die themselves are the same.
 155        """
 156        data = defaultdict(int)
 157        for (outcome_self, weight_self), (outcome_other,
 158                                          weight_other) in itertools.product(
 159                                              self.items(), other.items()):
 160            data[op(outcome_self, outcome_other, *args,
 161                    **kwargs)] += weight_self * weight_other
 162        return icepool.Die(data)

 378    def ppf_left(self, n, d=100):
 379        """Returns a quantile, `n / d` of the way through the cdf.
 380
 381        If the result lies between two outcomes, returns the lower of the two.
 382        """
 383        index = bisect.bisect_left(self.cweights(),
 384                                   (n * self.denominator() + d - 1) // d)
 385        if index >= self.num_outcomes():
 386            return self.max_outcome()
 387        return self.outcomes()[index]

 389    def ppf_right(self, n, d=100):
 390        """Returns a quantile, `n / d` of the way through the cdf.
 391
 392        If the result lies between two outcomes, returns the higher of the two.
 393        """
 394        index = bisect.bisect_right(self.cweights(),
 395                                    n * self.denominator() // d)
 396        if index >= self.num_outcomes():
 397            return self.max_outcome()
 398        return self.outcomes()[index]

 400    def ppf(self, n, d=100):
 401        """Returns a quantile, `n / d` of the way through the cdf.
 402
 403        If the result lies between two outcomes, returns the mean of the two.
 404        This will fail if the outcomes do not support division;
 405        in this case, use `ppf_left` or `ppf_right` instead.
 406        """
 407        return (self.ppf_left(n, d) + self.ppf_right(n, d)) / 2

 446        sd_i = self[i].standard_deviation()
 447        sd_j = self[j].standard_deviation()
 448        return self.covariance(i, j) / (sd_i * sd_j)

 493    def reroll(self, outcomes=None, *, max_depth=None):
 494        """Rerolls the given outcomes.
 495
 496        Args:
 497            outcomes: Selects which outcomes to reroll. Options:
 498                * A callable that takes an outcome and returns `True` if it
 499                    should be rerolled.
 500                * A set of outcomes to reroll.
 501                * If not provided, the min outcome will be rerolled.
 502            max_depth: The maximum number of times to reroll.
 503                If omitted, rerolls an unlimited number of times.
 504
 505        Returns:
 506            A die representing the reroll.
 507            If the reroll would never terminate, the result has no outcomes.
 508        """
 509        if outcomes is None:
 510            outcomes = {self.min_outcome()}
 511        elif callable(outcomes):
 512            outcomes = {
 513                outcome for outcome in self.outcomes() if outcomes(outcome)
 514            }
 515
 516        if max_depth is None:
 517            data = {
 518                outcome: weight
 519                for outcome, weight in self.items()
 520                if outcome not in outcomes
 521            }
 522        else:
 523            total_reroll_weight = sum(
 524                weight for outcome, weight in self.items()
 525                if outcome in outcomes)
 526            rerollable_factor = total_reroll_weight**max_depth
 527            stop_factor = (self.denominator()**max_depth +
 528                           total_reroll_weight**max_depth)
 529            data = {
 530                outcome:
 531                (rerollable_factor *
 532                 weight if outcome in outcomes else stop_factor * weight)
 533                for outcome, weight in self.items()
 534            }
 535        return icepool.Die(data)

 537    def reroll_until(self, outcomes, *, max_depth=None):
 538        """Rerolls until getting one of the given outcomes.
 539
 540        Essentially the complement of `reroll()`.
 541
 542        Args:
 543            outcomes: Selects which outcomes to reroll until. Options:
 544                * A callable that takes an outcome and returns `True` if it
 545                    should be accepted.
 546                * A set of outcomes to reroll until.
 547            max_depth: The maximum number of times to reroll.
 548                If omitted, rerolls an unlimited number of times.
 549
 550        Returns:
 551            A die representing the reroll.
 552            If the reroll would never terminate, the result has no outcomes.
 553        """
 554        if callable(outcomes):
 555            not_outcomes = {
 556                outcome for outcome in self.outcomes() if not outcomes(outcome)
 557            }
 558        else:
 559            not_outcomes = {
 560                not_outcome for not_outcome in self.outcomes()
 561                if not_outcome not in outcomes
 562            }
 563        return self.reroll(not_outcomes, max_depth=max_depth)

 565    def truncate(self, min_outcome=None, max_outcome=None):
 566        """Truncates the outcomes of this die to the given range.
 567
 568        The endpoints are included in the result if applicable.
 569        If one of the arguments is not provided, that side will not be truncated.
 570
 571        This effectively rerolls outcomes outside the given range.
 572        If instead you want to replace those outcomes with the nearest endpoint,
 573        use `clip()`.
 574
 575        Not to be confused with `trunc(die)`, which performs integer truncation
 576        on each outcome.
 577        """
 578        if min_outcome is not None:
 579            start = bisect.bisect_left(self.outcomes(), min_outcome)
 580        else:
 581            start = None
 582        if max_outcome is not None:
 583            stop = bisect.bisect_right(self.outcomes(), max_outcome)
 584        else:
 585            stop = None
 586        data = {k: v for k, v in self.items()[start:stop]}
 587        return icepool.Die(data)

 589    def clip(self, min_outcome=None, max_outcome=None):
 590        """Clips the outcomes of this die to the given values.
 591
 592        The endpoints are included in the result if applicable.
 593        If one of the arguments is not provided, that side will not be clipped.
 594
 595        This is not the same as rerolling outcomes beyond this range;
 596        the outcome is simply adjusted to fit within the range.
 597        This will typically cause some weight to bunch up at the endpoint.
 598        If you want to reroll outcomes beyond this range, use `truncate()`.
 599        """
 600        data = defaultdict(int)
 601        for outcome, weight in self.items():
 602            if min_outcome is not None and outcome <= min_outcome:
 603                data[min_outcome] += weight
 604            elif max_outcome is not None and outcome >= max_outcome:
 605                data[max_outcome] += weight
 606            else:
 607                data[outcome] += weight
 608        return icepool.Die(data)

 652    def sub(self, repl, /, *, max_depth=1, denominator_method='lcm'):
 653        """Changes outcomes of the die to other outcomes.
 654
 655        You can think of this as `sub`stituting outcomes of this die for other
 656        outcomes or dice. Or, as executing a `sub`routine based on the roll of
 657        this die.
 658
 659        Args:
 660            repl: One of the following:
 661                * A map from old outcomes to new outcomes.
 662                    Unmapped old outcomes stay the same.
 663                * A callable mapping old outcomes to new outcomes.
 664                The new outcomes may be dice rather than just single outcomes.
 665                The special value `icepool.Reroll` will reroll that old outcome.
 666            max_depth: `sub()` will be repeated with the same argument on the
 667                result this many times.
 668                If set to `None`, this will repeat until a fixed point is reached.
 669            denominator_method: As `icepool.Die()`.
 670
 671        Returns:
 672            The relabeled die.
 673        """
 674        if max_depth == 0:
 675            return self
 676        elif max_depth == 1:
 677            if hasattr(repl, 'items'):
 678                repl = [(repl[outcome] if outcome in repl else outcome)
 679                        for outcome in self.outcomes()]
 680            elif callable(repl):
 681                repl = [repl(outcome) for outcome in self.outcomes()]
 682
 683            return icepool.Die(*repl,
 684                               weights=self.weights(),
 685                               denominator_method=denominator_method)
 686        elif max_depth is not None:
 687            next = self.sub(repl,
 688                            max_depth=1,
 689                            denominator_method=denominator_method)
 690            return next.sub(repl,
 691                            max_depth=max_depth - 1,
 692                            denominator_method=denominator_method)
 693        else:
 694            # Seek fixed point.
 695            next = self.sub(repl,
 696                            max_depth=1,
 697                            denominator_method=denominator_method)
 698            if self.reduce().equals(next.reduce()):
 699                return self
 700            else:
 701                return next.sub(repl,
 702                                max_depth=None,
 703                                denominator_method=denominator_method)
 704
 705    def explode(self, outcomes=None, *, max_depth=None):
 706        """Causes outcomes to be rolled again and added to the total.
 707
 708        Args:
 709            outcomes: Which outcomes to explode. Options:
 710                * An iterable containing outcomes to explode.
 711                * A callable that takes an outcome and returns `True` if it
 712                    should be exploded.
 713                * If not supplied, the max outcome will explode.
 714            max_depth: The maximum number of additional dice to roll.
 715                If not supplied, a default value will be used.
 716        """
 717        if outcomes is None:
 718            outcomes = {self.max_outcome()}
 719        elif callable(outcomes):
 720            outcomes = {
 721                outcome for outcome in self.outcomes() if outcomes(outcome)
 722            }
 723
 724        if len(outcomes) == 0:
 725            return self
 726
 727        if max_depth is None:
 728            max_depth = 9
 729        elif max_depth < 0:
 730            raise ValueError('max_depth cannot be negative.')
 731        elif max_depth == 0:
 732            return self
 733
 734        tail_die = self.explode(outcomes=outcomes, max_depth=max_depth - 1)
 735
 736        def sub_func(outcome):
 737            if outcome in outcomes:
 738                return outcome + tail_die
 739            else:
 740                return outcome
 741
 742        return self.sub(sub_func, denominator_method='lcm')

 761    def lowest(*dice):
 762        """Roll all the dice and take the lowest.
 763
 764        The maximum outcome is equal to the highest maximum outcome among all
 765        input dice.
 766        """
 767        dice = [Die(die) for die in dice]

 770        dice = icepool.align(*dice)
 771        sweights = tuple(
 772            math.prod(t) for t in zip(*(die.sweights() for die in dice)))
 773        return icepool.from_sweights(dice[0].outcomes(), sweights)

 789    @cached_property
 790    def _repeat_and_sum_cache(self):
 791        return {}
 792
 793    def repeat_and_sum(self, num_dice):
 794        """Roll this die `num_dice` times and sum the results.
 795
 796        If `num_dice` is negative, roll the die `abs(num_dice)` times and negate
 797        the result.
 798        """
 799        if num_dice in self._repeat_and_sum_cache:
 800            return self._repeat_and_sum_cache[num_dice]
 801
 802        if num_dice < 0:
 803            result = -self.repeat_and_sum(-num_dice)
 804        elif num_dice == 0:
 805            result = self.zero()
 806        elif num_dice == 1:
 807            result = self
 808        else:
 809            # Binary split seems to perform much worse.
 810            result = self + self.repeat_and_sum(num_dice - 1)
 811
 812        self._repeat_and_sum_cache[num_dice] = result
 813        return result

 815    def d(self, other):
 816        """Roll the left die, then roll the right die that many times and sum the outcomes.
 817
 818        If an `int` is provided for the right side, it becomes a standard die
 819        with that many faces. Otherwise it is cast to a die.
 820        """
 821        if isinstance(other, int):
 822            other = icepool.standard(other)
 823        else:
 824            other = icepool.Die(other)
 825
 826        data = defaultdict(int)
 827
 828        max_abs_die_count = max(abs(self.min_outcome()),
 829                                abs(self.max_outcome()))
 830        for die_count, die_count_weight in self.items():
 831            factor = other.denominator()**(max_abs_die_count - abs(die_count))
 832            subresult = other.repeat_and_sum(die_count)
 833            for outcome, subresult_weight in subresult.items():
 834                data[outcome] += subresult_weight * die_count_weight * factor
 835
 836        return icepool.Die(data)

 843             num_dice=None,
 844             count_dice=None,
 845             *,
 846             truncate_min=None,
 847             truncate_max=None):
 848        """Roll several of this die and sum some or all of the sorted results.
 849
 850        Args:
 851            num_dice: The number of dice to roll. All dice will have the same
 852                outcomes as `self`.
 853            count_dice: Only dice selected by this will be counted.
 854                See `DicePool.count_dice()` for details.
 855            truncate_min: A sequence of one outcome per die.
 856                That die will be truncated to that minimum outcome, with all
 857                lower outcomes being removed (i.e. rerolled).

 865            A Die representing the probability distribution of the sum.
 866        """

 868                            num_dice,

 870                            truncate_min=truncate_min,
 871                            truncate_max=truncate_max)
 872        if isinstance(count_dice, int):
 873            return pool
 874        else:
 875            return pool.sum()

An immutable mapping of outcomes to nonnegative int weights.

Dice are immutable. Methods do not modify the die in-place; rather they return a die representing the result.

It is (mostly) well-defined to have a die with zero-weight outcomes, even though this is not a proper probability distribution. These can be useful in a few cases, such as:

  • DicePool and EvalPool will iterate through zero-weight outcomes with 0 count, rather than None or skipping that outcome.
  • icepool.align() and the like are convenient for making dice share the same set of outcomes.

However, zero-weight outcomes have a computational cost like any other outcome. Unless you have a specific use case in mind, it's best to leave them out.

Most operators and methods will not introduce zero-weight outcomes if their arguments do not have any.

It's also possible to have "empty" dice with no outcomes at all, though these have little use other than being sentinel values.
